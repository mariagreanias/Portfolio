Cardinal Color Group.

Reimagining a legacy printing company’s digital presence into a modern, conversion-focused marketing platform. The new experience prioritizes clarity, positions the brand as a strategic partner, and creates a seamless path from discovery to inquiry.

Project Overview

Cardinal Color Group is a long-standing commercial printing and marketing communications company with a broad range of services, including digital printing, packaging, direct mail, and fulfillment. Their capabilities span both traditional production and data-driven marketing solutions, making them more than just a print vendor.

However, their previous website did not reflect this depth. The experience felt dated, with limited hierarchy, unclear messaging, and little distinction between services. Visitors were often left unsure of what Cardinal truly specialized in or how to engage with them.

This redesign aimed to reposition the company as a modern marketing partner. The goal was not only to improve aesthetics, but to create a structured, strategic experience that communicates expertise, builds trust, and drives measurable business outcomes.

The Problem

The original website functioned primarily as a static brochure, lacking the strategic elements needed to convert visitors into leads. Key services were buried or poorly explained, and there was no clear narrative guiding users through the company’s offerings.

Navigation was inconsistent, making it difficult for users to find relevant information quickly. Messaging was generic and failed to communicate Cardinal’s differentiators, particularly their ability to integrate print with data-driven marketing.

As a result, the site underperformed as a business tool—users visited but did not engage, and opportunities for lead generation were frequently lost due to unclear calls-to-action and weak information architecture.

Website Transformation

The redesign introduced a clear content hierarchy and simplified navigation system, allowing users to quickly understand Cardinal’s services and value. Each section was intentionally structured to guide users from awareness to action.

Service pages were reworked to highlight benefits rather than just features, using concise messaging and supporting visuals. Strategic calls-to-action were placed throughout the experience to encourage inquiries without disrupting flow.

The result is a cohesive, intuitive platform that improves usability while reinforcing credibility at every touchpoint.

Before — cluttered layout, unclear hierarchy

After — clean structure, clear messaging, improved flow

Strategy

The core strategy focused on repositioning Cardinal from a production vendor to a full-service marketing partner. This required a shift in messaging—from listing capabilities to demonstrating outcomes and value.

Content was reframed around client needs, emphasizing personalization, data integration, and measurable results. The structure supports multiple user types, from marketing managers seeking campaign support to procurement teams evaluating production capabilities.

By aligning messaging, structure, and user flow, the site now communicates a clear and compelling narrative that supports both brand perception and business growth.

Visual Design

The visual system is intentionally minimal, allowing content and messaging to take priority. Strong typography establishes hierarchy and guides the user’s attention, while generous spacing improves readability and creates a sense of sophistication.

Imagery was selected to reflect scale, precision, and real-world application of services. Subtle color accents are used strategically to highlight key elements and calls-to-action without overwhelming the interface.

Overall, the design balances professionalism with modern simplicity, reinforcing trust while ensuring the experience feels current and accessible.

Results

Following the redesign, the website demonstrated measurable improvements in both user engagement and lead generation. Clearer messaging and improved usability contributed to stronger performance across key metrics.
